## Design Context

### Users

**Storefront (customers):** Local community members browsing for nearby farm produce — not power shoppers, but curious explorers looking for local gems. They're on their phone at a farmers market or at home on a weekend morning. They want to feel like they're discovering something genuine, not shopping on Amazon. Trust and authenticity matter more than speed.

**Dashboard (farm owners):** Non-technical farmers managing their own products and orders. They need clarity, large targets, and plain language. The dashboard should feel like a well-organized ledger, not a SaaS tool.

### Brand Personality

**3 words: Rustic · Cozy · Welcoming**

The interface should feel like stepping into a farmhouse kitchen — warm lighting, worn wood, handwritten labels, and the smell of something baking. Not "country kitsch" with tractors and burlap, but genuinely lived-in and intentional. Think curated general store meets editorial food magazine.

**Emotional goals:** Customers should feel discovered, not sold to. The interface should evoke trust, warmth, and the quiet satisfaction of buying directly from someone who grew what you're eating.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme:** Light mode only. Cream backgrounds (#fcf9f0), Forest Green (#173809), Terracotta (#a03f29) accents. No dark mode.
- **Typography:** Newsreader (italic, editorial) for all headlines. Plus Jakarta Sans for utility text. Headlines should feel like a hand-set broadsheet — generous, unhurried.
- **Texture:** Subtle grain overlay (already implemented at 3% opacity) is the right call — keep it.
- **Photography:** Images are the product. Give them room to breathe. Asymmetric placements, slight rotations, overlapping frames — all reinforce the handmade feel.
- **Motion:** Slow and deliberate. No bounce or elastic. Gentle scale/fade transitions that don't rush the user.

**Anti-references:**
- No AI aesthetic fingerprints: no cyan-on-dark, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero metric grids
- No big-box grocery app feel (Instacart, Walmart Grocery) — avoid clinical white layouts, aggressive CTAs, discount badge spam
- No generic "farm" clichés — no tractor icons, no plaid patterns, no over-saturated green

### Design Principles

1. **Discovered, not sold to** — Copy and layout should feel like browsing a market stall, not a conversion funnel. Avoid urgency patterns.
2. **Texture over perfection** — Slight asymmetry, organic rotations, and tonal layering over rigid grids and drop shadows. The grain overlay, offset images, and tonal surface shifts are load-bearing — never flatten them.
3. **Every surface has a story** — Section transitions should feel like turning a page, not clicking a tab. Use background washes (surface → surface-container-low) to create narrative flow.
4. **Restraint is warmth** — Generous whitespace, unhurried type sizing, and minimal required interaction. Don't cram. A sparse page reads as confident, not incomplete.
5. **Genuine over polished** — Imperfect feels trustworthy here. A slightly italic headline, a photo that bleeds off the card, a label in small caps — these details signal craft, not automation.
